# Focus Map

A practical state map for the Sound Lab.

This file defines the first-pass target feel, audio behaviour, and suggested exercise types for each shell.

---

## Focus 10

### State Intent
Mind quiet enough to observe.
Body heavy enough to drift.
The listener should feel physically settled without losing awareness.

### Felt Qualities
- body load
- warmth
- stillness
- inward attention
- reduced urge to move

### Audio Behaviour
- soft, stable bed
- restrained pulse introduction
- minimal high-frequency sparkle
- little spatial drama
- very low novelty once plateau starts

### Best Uses
- release
- body settling
- pre-sleep exploration
- recovery
- basic induction

### Good Prompt Style
- body-first
- sparse
- simple
- reassuring but not sentimental

### Failure Modes
- too sleepy too fast
- muddy audio
- overlong induction
- guidance talking too much

---

## Focus 12

### State Intent
Awareness opens outward while staying anchored.
The listener should feel more spacious, perceptive, and mentally clear than in F10.

### Felt Qualities
- expansion
- air
- openness
- increased mental room
- light alertness

### Audio Behaviour
- wider stereo field
- more air in the bed
- slightly more brightness
- subtle motion cues
- pulse remains supportive, not dominant

### Best Uses
- problem solving
- reflection
- intuitive imagery
- journaling prep
- perceptual tasks

### Good Prompt Style
- one question, then silence
- observational
- spacious
- non-forcing

### Failure Modes
- too much brightness
- cognitive tension
- becoming “awake and analytical” instead of open

---

## Focus 15

### State Intent
Reduce time-markers.
The listener should feel less clock-bound and less aware of progression.

### Felt Qualities
- suspension
- smoothness
- timelessness
- low event density
- drift without collapse

### Audio Behaviour
- very gradual transitions
- almost no obvious landmarks
- softened pulse articulation
- low rhythmic information
- broad, stable field

### Best Uses
- deep stillness
- patterning
- extended nonverbal practice
- timeless contemplation

### Good Prompt Style
- minimal
- spacious
- light touch
- fewer reminders than other shells

### Failure Modes
- boredom
- unintended sleep
- too much movement revealing time passage

---

## Focus 18

### State Intent
A coherent, warm, relationally open state.
The listener should feel emotionally brightened without being pushed into artificial bliss.

### Felt Qualities
- warmth
- affection
- coherence
- tenderness
- uplift

### Audio Behaviour
- slightly richer harmonic colour
- gentle warmth in tonal bed
- smoother top-end
- a sense of soft radiance rather than heaviness

### Best Uses
- compassion practice
- gratitude
- relational repair
- heart-led imagery
- soothing after hard sessions

### Good Prompt Style
- emotionally simple
- grounded
- direct
- no syrup

### Failure Modes
- New Age cheese
- sentimental overload
- too much musicality making it performative

---

## Focus 21

### State Intent
A bridge-state.
The listener should feel at an edge, crossing, or threshold while remaining stable and able to report.

### Felt Qualities
- liminality
- depth
- distance
- dimensionality
- threshold awareness

### Audio Behaviour
- more depth cues
- more subtle perceptual ambiguity
- restrained but noticeable sense of “elsewhere”
- spatial layers must remain clean

### Best Uses
- threshold work
- symbolic exploration
- advanced free-flow sessions
- edge-of-sleep observation
- nonverbal journeys

### Good Prompt Style
- light framing at the start
- very little interference during plateau
- clear return at the end

### Failure Modes
- theatrical eeriness
- listener destabilization
- too many effects
- becoming cinematic instead of experiential

# Initial Preset Notes

## F10 Preset Notes
- use the softest bed
- keep motion minimal
- treat the plateau as a held stillness

## F12 Preset Notes
- open the stereo field
- add subtle air
- allow a little more lucidity

## F15 Preset Notes
- reduce event density
- avoid clear section edges
- let time smear

## F18 Preset Notes
- warmth without melodrama
- add tonal kindness, not triumph

## F21 Preset Notes
- add depth, not chaos
- keep the threshold clean and navigable

---

# Zener Extension Notes

F12 is the best first shell for Zener work because it balances:
- calm
- alertness
- openness
- reportability

Secondary candidates:
- F10 for deep calm / low-pressure guessing
- F15 for very slow image-first trials

Avoid starting Zener work in F21.
Threshold states are interesting, but they add too much ambiguity too early.

---

# Design Reminder

These shells are not dogma.
They are operating targets.

If a preset produces the target feel consistently, it belongs.
If it does not, it gets revised.
